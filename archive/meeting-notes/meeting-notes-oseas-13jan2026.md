## Vision Presentation Feedback

### What Oseas Liked
- **Direction is good** - moves away from vulnerable "just a chatbot" position
- **Visuals are excellent** - communicates the vision clearly in minutes
- Asked how they were made (Nanobanana/Gemini, ~2 hours with setup)
- Sees value in behavior change approach, aligns with company needs

### Oseas's Concern: User Willingness

> "Sigo teniendo esta impresión de que si el producto está diseñado para ayudarle a personas a que hagan un proceso de crecimiento de propia voluntad... nos pone en una situación muy vulnerable, en tanto en cuanto no es lo normal que las personas hagamos eso y que seamos consistentes."

**His question:** Can we make something more "passive" that:
- Gives companies faster, more visible results
- Requires less active effort from users

This is why he's been curious about **Team Performance** angle - work with 10 team leaders instead of 500 individuals.

### Ruy's Response

1. **Team Performance has the same problem** - still requires leaders to want to change
2. **Key insight:** Anchor to things people actually care about:
   - Avoiding problems ("te recuerdo que respires tres veces para que no la cagues")
   - Getting promotions
   - Performance reviews
   - Not having conflicts
3. **Make it genuinely useful for work**, not just "be a better person"
4. **HCI research exists** on how to help people who said they want to change actually do it
5. **Two directions to increase stickiness:**
   - Help with the "it" of the job (implementation, plans, not just soft skills)
   - Extend to personal life (relationships, health, parenting) - becomes your "Pepe Grillo" for everything

### Buyer Question
Oseas raised: Will L&D buyers who built careers buying courses see value in this? They've never been fired for buying another course.

**Ruy's response:** If Applied Learning positioning works, this is Applied Learning on steroids - you don't just teach, you ensure it happens.
