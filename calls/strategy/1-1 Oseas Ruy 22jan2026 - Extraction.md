## 1. Design Principles

### DP1: Individual is the Mechanism, Team is the Product

> "Cambia mucho la manera de poder abordar la cuestión comercial si el framing desde el principio es que tenemos una solución que se llama Team Performance."

Don't sell "individual coaching." Sell "Team Performance" — individual coaching is how you deliver it.

**Why this matters:**
- "Coaching" has budget/compliance blockers
- "Team Performance" has clearer organizational value
- Same work, different framing, better commercial traction

**Implication for product:** Position everything under Team Performance umbrella. Individual coaching is a component, not the headline.

---

### DP2: Social Context Increases Behavior Change Adherence

> "Una de las cosas que ayudan a la adhesión del cambio de comportamiento es el entorno social... Si estamos todos en equipo haciéndolo, está un poquito más cabrón."

People change behavior better when:
- Others are watching
- There's shared commitment
- Progress is visible to peers
- It's "responsibility to team" not "personal development"

**Implication for product:** Build social accountability into the coaching experience. Team dashboard showing adoption/progress. Individual work framed as team contribution.

---

### DP3: You Must Work Both Levels

> "Para hacer un high performing team tienes a huevo que pasar por las dos cosas — no nada más puedes hacerle intervenciones al equipo sin darle nada al individuo."

This confirms what Dolo and Horacio said: team-only interventions don't work. Individual development is required.

**The sequence question:**
- Current: Team assessment → Individual assessment → Individual coaching (in team context)
- Future: Could flip to Team first, but individual work is always needed

---

### DP4: Start Where You're Stronger

> "Para team effectiveness... no está tan codificada como la metodología para dar coaching individual."

Ruy's assessment: Individual coaching methodology is more documented, more achievable to digitize. Team methodology is contextual, less codified, harder to automate.

**Implication:** Start with individual coaching (where we're stronger), build toward team (where we need to learn).

## 3. Product Ideas

### ID1: Team Survey + CB Assessment Combination

> "Hay un instrumento que tiene Axialent... los cuatro pilares de Katzenbach... con eso puedes entender dónde está el equipo versus el ideal."

**The combination:**
1. Team Survey (4-Quadrant) → WHERE team is broken
2. CB Assessment → Individual patterns
3. Cross-analysis → "Your team is weak on X, you're contributing via Y"

**Why it works:**
- Both instruments exist
- Cross-referencing is analysis, not coding
- Creates clear "so what" for individuals

---

### ID2: Team Progress Dashboard

> "Eso sí se puede hacer muy fácil, un dashboard de cómo le ha ido al equipo."

Show:
- Adoption (who's doing coaching sessions)
- Topics being worked on (connected to team gaps)
- Progress over time

**Why it matters:** Makes individual work visible at team level. Creates social accountability.

---

### ID3: Leader-First Pathway

> "Ayúdale al líder del equipo a que esté menos jodido individualmente para que pueda autofacilitar un par de cosas a su equipo."

When team is too broken to self-facilitate:
1. Coach the leader first
2. Leader gains awareness + skills
3. Leader facilitates basic team conversations (charter, roles)
4. Then broader team development

**Use case:** Dysfunctional teams that can't even have productive conversations yet.

---

### ID4: Hybrid HPT Package

> "Agarramos el HPT, lo digitalizamos, le bajamos dos rayitas para que sea menos complicado, y lo empieces a vender."

Package:
- Team Survey + CB Assessment (digital)
- Teaching sessions (Leo or Horacio facilitates)
- AI coaching for individuals (required, weekly)
- Occasional group coaching (Horacio)

**Cheaper than full HPT** because:
- Digital assessments (no consultant time)
- AI handles individual coaching (scaled)
- Human facilitator only for group moments

## 4. Commercial Insights

### The Adherence Problem

> "Somos horribles para cambiar... esto es como querer vender dietas que la gente sí siga cuando nadie puede seguir una puta dieta."

Individual behavior change has low adherence. The team framing helps because:
- External accountability
- Visible to peers
- Part of job responsibility (not personal choice)

### The Budget Problem

Coaching alone faces:
- No budget category
- Compliance blockers
- Unclear ROI

Team Performance faces:
- HR/L&D budget exists
- Team outcomes are measurable
- Clearer business case

## Synthesis for Team Effectiveness Vision

**What this call clarifies:**

1. **Commercial framing matters.** "Team Performance" not "coaching." Same product, different positioning.

2. **Social accountability helps adherence.** Individual coaching in team context > individual coaching alone.

3. **Start with individual, build to team.** We're stronger on individual methodology. Build pieces that extend to team.

4. **Quick win is possible.** Team Survey + CB Assessment + human facilitation + AI coaching. Prototype now, minimal dev.

5. **Leader-first pathway** for dysfunctional teams. Coach leader → leader facilitates basics → then broader team.

6. **Budget reality constrains options.** Whatever we build needs to show results fast with minimal investment.
