### 1.2 Praxis (Observed Practice)

Knowledge from observing how experienced coaches actually work.

**From Horacio (Jan 2026):**

**Core Philosophy:**
> "Las sesiones no pueden ser tratadas como eventos aislados... es una larga conversación repartida en seis momentos."

Sessions are NOT isolated events. One continuous conversation distributed across moments.

> "Es una gran responsabilidad velar por hacer todas las conexiones posibles con las cosas que se han conversado antes."

The coach's responsibility: make ALL connections to prior conversations.

**Note-Taking Practice:**
- During Session: Takes detailed notes while coaching; informs coachee when doing so (ICF principle)
- Post-Session: 20-30 minutes for clean-up; Word document per coachee, chronological; includes personal notes/reminders
- Pre-Session: 15-minute review of all prior session notes - "Leo rápidamente cómo fue fluyendo la cosa"

**Memory & Continuity Benefits:**
- Builds Trust: "Si yo soy coachee y me encuentro con un coach que en la tercera sesión me cita algo que yo le comenté en la primera, lo primero que siento es que este tipo me escuchó."
- Saves Time: No need to re-ask questions already answered
- Tracks Examples by Name: If coachee mentions "Julián" in session 3, coach recalls "Julián from session 2 who..."

**"Yellow Lights"** - Personal reminders for future exploration:
- "Veo riesgo de que se pierda en generalidades"
- "Tener en cuenta indagar a fondo su preocupación sobre las malinterpretaciones"

**Handling Resistance:**
1. Don't defend tools extensively
2. Acknowledge statistical questions exist
3. Pivot to: "El valor que esto tiene es abrir entre nosotros una conversación sobre cosas que sean válidas para vos"
4. If they want to abandon diagnostic: "Me entrego yo como coach al coaching de lo que él quiera priorizar"

**Challenge Interventions:**
- Distinguish Coaching-to-Solve vs Coaching-to-Develop: "¿Hasta qué punto esas estrategias implicaban conciencia sobre el desarrollo del otro, o más bien, intervenciones directivas que resuelvan los problemas del momento?"
- Closing the Loop: "Use la pregunta: ¿qué harías distinto la próxima vez?"

**Session Structure Flexibility:**
- Sometimes no explicit commitment (mindset shift is value)
- Sometimes technical advice ("ya no tanto de coaching" but advisory)
- Always: Schedule next session before ending current one

## 2. The Coaching Process Structure

### 2.1 Setup Phase

**Questions for interviews:**
- How do you set up a coaching engagement?
- What information do you gather before the first session?
- How do you handle "reunion cero" (meeting zero) with the boss?
- What diagnostics do you use and why?

**Observations from Horacio:**

**The Spectrum of Engagements:**
- *Coaching as standalone product:* Higher likelihood of coachee buy-in; person/company specifically requests coaching
- *Coaching as part of larger program:* May trigger "victimous" positioning - "la empresa me dice que vaya"

**Key insight:** The coachee's predisposition and sense of personal need significantly impacts coaching effectiveness.

**Reunion Cero (Meeting Zero):**
- Meeting between coach, coachee, and coachee's boss
- Sets objectives together
- Boss states expectations: "Espero que mejores tu capacidad de escucha"
- These become reference points throughout the process

**Diagnostic Instruments:**
- LSI (Life Styles Inventory) - preferred for culture projects (speaks same language as OCI)
- CB Assessment Tool - increasingly used
- Strong opinion: "En cualquier lugar que tú encajes el coaching... si le falta el diagnóstico individual, te estás perdiendo una enorme oportunidad de tocar la fibra de la conciencia de la persona"

**Pre-Diagnostic Question (powerful technique):**
Before showing any instrument results:
> "Si tú estuvieras hablando en confianza ahora con un amigo en una mesa de café... ¿qué oportunidades de mejora reconoces que tienes en tu forma de liderar?"

Opens the door for honest self-reflection before seeing data.

### 2.2 Session Flow

**Questions for interviews:**
- How do you structure a typical session?
- How do you balance following the coachee's agenda vs. your observations?
- When do you give advice vs. ask questions?
- How do you handle unexpected topics?

**Observations from Horacio:**

**Opening - Context Gathering:**
- Age, family situation, education
- Professional background
- Prior coaching experience

**Mid-Session Dynamics:**
- Watch for "success story sharing" that may mask real needs
- Note when coachee speaks at high level, struggles to get to detail
- Track mid-process revelations that change understanding (e.g., boss had requested something months ago)

**Challenge Interventions:**
- Distinguish helping solve a problem vs. developing the person
- "¿Eso fue una ayuda para resolver un problema o una acción de desarrollo?"

**Closing:**
- May have explicit commitment or not (mindset shift counts as value)
- Always schedule next session before ending

### 2.3 Cross-Session Continuity

**Questions for interviews:**
- How do you prepare for follow-up sessions?
- How do you track commitments and follow up?
- How do you connect current topics to prior conversations?
- What do you do when coachee hasn't done "homework"?

**Observations from Horacio:**

**Pre-Session Preparation:**
- 15-minute review of all prior session notes
- Arrive with full context ready

**Tracking & Connecting:**
- Connect current topics to diagnostic findings
- Reference prior examples by name: "Ah sí, el que me contaste la vez pasada"
- Track commitments: "Hizo los deberes" - did they do the homework?

**Making Connections Explicit:**
> "Si yo veo que entran muy en zona de luz algunos [objetivos] y otros quedan en sombra, digo: ¿te estás dando cuenta que estos están quedando en sombra y no los estamos atendiendo?"

## 3. Temporal Dimension

### 3.1 Single Session Arc

**From Horacio's Case Study:**

A single session can include:
- Opening with progress report or new topic
- Exploration and discovery
- Challenge intervention (when appropriate)
- Advisory/tips (when appropriate)
- Commitment or mindset shift
- Scheduling next session

**Flexibility is key:** Not every session needs explicit action items.

### 3.2 Multi-Session Arc (6-Session Example)

**From Horacio's Case Study:**

| Session | Theme | Key Moments |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| Pre-coaching | LSI Feedback | Pre-diagnostic question; blind spots discovery; initial commitments |
| 1 | Resistance & Pivot | Coachee questions tool validity; coach pivots to coachee's priorities |
| 2 | Succession Planning | Challenge intervention; mid-process revelation about boss's request |
| 3 | Progress + New Topics | Matrix completed; new development areas emerge; advisory mode used |
| 4-6 | (Not detailed) | Continuation of themes |

**Pattern:** Topics weave through sessions; new information can reframe earlier understanding.

### 3.3 Long-term Relationship

**Organizational Context:**

In culture transformation projects:
- C-Level starts first
- N-2 follows with 1-2 month delay
- Cascading continues

**Why intentional delay?**
> "Si tú eres un C-Level y alguien que te reporta a ti lo empezara exactamente al mismo tiempo... se pierde la persona que te reporta a ti la oportunidad de volver a ti cuando él hace su experiencia de aprendizaje encontrándote a ti ya con un poco de camino recorrido."

**Multi-touchpoint processes:**
- Workshops (knowledge transfer)
- Coaching (anchors learning to specific reality)
- Facilitaciones (in-situ feedback or structured team sessions)

## 4. Observations with Design Implications

These principles emerged from Horacio's interview with explicit relevance for AI coaching:

### 4.1 Memory is Non-Negotiable
> "Una IA que haga coaching tiene que ser una IA con memoria, no puede ser una IA que empiece cada sesión olvidándose del contexto previo."

**Implication:** AI coaching must maintain rich, persistent memory across all interactions.

### 4.2 Track Examples by Name
If coachee mentions "Julian" in session 3, AI should recall "Julian from session 2 who received feedback about one-page reports..."

**Implication:** Entity recognition and linking across sessions; people, projects, situations should be tracked as named objects.

### 4.3 Pre-Session Context Loading
Review full history before each interaction - "Leo rápidamente cómo fue fluyendo la cosa"

**Implication:** System should load/summarize relevant context before interaction begins.

### 4.4 Yellow Light Reminders
Flag topics for future exploration:
- Risk patterns observed
- Unexplored mentions
- Connections to diagnostic data

**Implication:** System should track "open loops" and surface them appropriately.

### 4.5 Connect Diagnostic to Live Examples
When coachee mentions behavior/concern, link to diagnostic findings where relevant.

**Implication:** Maintain connections between assessment data and behavioral observations.

### 4.6 Qualitative Data Capture
Consider interview-based intake - richer than questionnaires alone.
> "Es lo más poderoso que yo he visto para que la persona transite el coaching con conciencia y compromiso."

**Implication:** AI-conducted qualitative interviews could be more powerful than forms.

### 4.7 Recognize Deflection Patterns
Success story sharing may mask real needs:
> "Una forma hasta inconsciente en que un coachee no te dice qué es lo que realmente necesita como ayuda."

**Implication:** System should recognize when user is sharing wins vs. exploring challenges.

### 4.8 Support Both Modes
- Advisory/tips (technical guidance, best practices)
- True coaching (probing questions, mindset shifts, challenges)

**Implication:** AI should have distinct modes and know when each is appropriate.

### 4.9 Track Commitments
Follow up on prior session outcomes:
- "Hizo los deberes" - did they do the homework?
- Reference what was committed to

**Implication:** System must track commitments and surface them for follow-up.

### 4.10 Make Connections Explicit
> "Si yo veo que entran muy en zona de luz algunos [objetivos] y otros quedan en sombra, digo: ¿te estás dando cuenta que estos están quedando en sombra y no los estamos atendiendo?"

**Implication:** System should notice imbalances and point them out proactively.

## 5. Open Questions

Questions to explore in future interviews and research:

### Process Questions
- How do coaches decide when to challenge vs. support?
- What signals indicate a coachee is ready for deeper work?
- How do coaches handle coachees who consistently avoid commitments?
- What determines session frequency and total duration of engagement?

### Memory Questions
- What's the minimum viable memory for effective coaching?
- How do coaches decide what to remember vs. let go?
- What's the balance between recalling context and allowing fresh starts?

### Assessment Questions
- When are quantitative assessments valuable vs. limiting?
- How do coaches handle assessment results that don't match coachee's self-perception?
- What qualitative methods are most effective for building awareness?

### System Questions
- How does individual coaching connect to team/organizational change?
- What role does the boss/sponsor play throughout the process?
- How do coaches maintain confidentiality while demonstrating value to sponsors?

## Appendix: Intervention Types Reference

From Horacio's framework:

### Workshops (Talleres)
- Transfer of knowledge, exercises, practices
- Training modality
- Limitation: "El taller por sí mismo nunca es suficiente"

### Coaching
- Anchors workshop learning to specific reality
- Without it, learning "se evapora en 48 horas"
- Always starts with individual diagnostic

### Facilitaciones

**Type A: In-Situ Business Meeting Facilitation**
- Observer in real business meetings
- Permission to intervene and provide feedback
- Points out: listening issues, interruptions, victim language

**Type B: High Performing Teams (HPT)**
- Structured team development sessions
- Session 1: Clarify objectives, connect team to individual goals
- Session 2: Define processes, decision mechanisms
