Horacio walks Ruy through his detailed coaching notes from a real 6-session executive coaching engagement, explaining his process session-by-session. The conversation reveals critical insights about how professional coaches track context, build relationships, and create value through memory and continuity. ## 2. LSI Feedback Session (Pre-Coaching)
**Status:** Detailed example provided

**Process revealed:**
1. **Context gathering first:** Ask coachee about background, career, prior coaching experience
2. **Pre-diagnostic question:** "If you were speaking honestly with a friend at a coffee table, what improvement opportunities do you recognize in your leadership style?"
3. **Explain LSI framework:** Coach explains the logic/structure of the diagnostic
4. **Invite self-analysis:** Coachee examines own results, gives first reactions
5. **Track reactions:** Coach notes where coachee agrees/disagrees with diagnostic

**Coachee profile (anonymized):**
- Engineering background, MBA, married, two children from first marriage
- Third coaching process in career
- Self-identified weakness: conflict management causing anxiety
- LSI showed high "avoidance" style (green) - aligned with self-assessment
- **Key insight from diagnostic:** All defensive styles appeared as "blind spots" - others see him as more aggressive (red) than he sees himself

**Coach's technique:**
- Takes careful notes even when coachee minimizes issues ("I don't avoid, I resolve internally")
- Creates "yellow light" reminders for future exploration
- Noted risk: "This person may get lost in generalizations, viewing things too holistically"

## 3. Session 1 - First Coaching Session
**Status:** Detailed example provided

**Opening dynamic:**
- Coachee returned still questioning LSI results - "doesn't match my self-image"
- Consulted with a previous coach who suggested methodology might be flawed
- Previous coach's advice: "detach from results, enjoy the coaching process"

**How coach handled resistance:**
- Acknowledged concern but reframed: value is in the conversation, not defending the tool
- "If you don't want to use the LSI diagnostic, I'll coach whatever you want to prioritize"

**New agenda emerged:**
- Coachee wants help developing a succession plan for his team (HR requested this)
- Coach initially confused - why bring this to coaching?
- Through inquiry, coach discovered real request: validation of his "leader-as-coach" approach

**Pattern identified by coach:**
- Coachee spent session sharing success stories (Julián example: gave feedback, person improved)
- This may be unconscious deflection from actual needs
- Coach notes: "He started by telling me what he's doing well"

**Mindset shift achieved:**
- Discussion about micro vs macro leadership styles
- Insight: Someone who responds well to macro-direction won't necessarily become a macro-directing leader themselves
- To predict future leadership style, need to observe reportees leading others

**Session close:**
- No explicit commitment, but valuable perspective shift delivered
- Coach noted: session added value through the insight, even without formal action item

## 4. Session 2 - Continuing Succession Plan Theme
**Status:** Detailed example provided

**Session structure:**
- Returned to succession planning topic from Session 1
- Coachee described his development strategies for different reportees:
  - **Julián:** Empowerment works, responds to macro-instructions
  - **Maxi:** Needs micro-direction, lacks autonomy (potential exit if no improvement)

**Coach's intervention on Josafar case:**
- Coachee "landed helicopter" to help Josafar solve immediate crisis
- Coach challenged: "Did you help solve a problem, or develop him as a leader?"

**Key revelation:**

- This changes the picture: there's external pressure, not just personal development goal
- Coach now understands political dimension (how will coachee look to boss?)

**Session outcome:**
- Coachee declared ready to create first draft of succession plan
- Committed to bring it to next session
- Feedback: "Through this coaching I'm closing topics better" (valued the structure)

## 7. Critical Insight: Memory and Continuity
**Status:** Key product design insight

**Horacio's philosophy:**
> "The sessions cannot be treated as isolated events. They're one long conversation distributed across six moments."

**Why this matters:**
- Coach responsibility: make all possible connections to previous conversations
- If coachee mentions "Julián" in Session 3, coach should recall "Julián from Session 2 who..."
- This builds trust: "This person listened to me, remembers what I said"
- Saves time: don't need to re-ask same questions

**Horacio's practice:**
- Takes detailed notes specifically to remember examples, names, commitments
- Reviews notes before each session to refresh context
- Can reference: "Remember in Session 1 you identified 10 topics?"

**AI product implication:**
> "An AI doing this coaching must be an AI with memory. It cannot start each session forgetting prior context. This should be easier for AI than humans - we can forget."

## 8. Note-Taking Practices
**Status:** Process insight

**Horacio's approach:**
- Long Word document per coachee, chronological
- Very detailed in some engagements (like this one), lighter in others
- Always enough to remember examples, names, key points
- Includes personal reminders: "yellow light to explore later"

**ICF guideline mentioned:**
- If taking notes, inform coachee (especially if looking away on video)
- Frame positively: "I take notes so I remember better next time"

**If coachee objects to notes:**
- Coach explains tradeoff: "Without notes, I may need to re-ask things"
- Most coachees accept when benefit is explained
