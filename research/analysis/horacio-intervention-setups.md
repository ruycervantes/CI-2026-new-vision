# Client Context & Intervention Setups

**Source:** Interview with Horacio - "Entendimiento de procesos de coaching"
**Date:** January 2026

---

## 1. The Spectrum of Coaching Engagements

Horacio frames coaching as existing on a spectrum based on how it's positioned:

### Coaching as Standalone Product
- Person/company specifically requests coaching
- Higher likelihood of coachee buy-in
- "Hay una especie de prediagnóstico interno de la compañía, que a veces la misma persona lo pide, o si no, es el jefe de la persona, o es recursos humanos que le recomienda al jefe"
- Coachee arrives already knowing or accepting they need the support

### Coaching as Part of Larger Program
- Culture transformation, leadership development
- May trigger "victimous" positioning: "Bueno, la empresa me dice que vaya a este programa y la empresa me pide que haga sesiones de coaching"
- Less favorable for coaching success when person participates only because they're "disciplined to a decision made by others"

**Key insight:** The coachee's predisposition and sense of personal need significantly impacts coaching effectiveness.

## 2. Culture Transformation Projects (Largest Scale)

These are the most comprehensive engagements:

### Diagnostic Phase
- **OCI (Organizational Culture Inventory)** - quantitative, statistical
- Two photographs taken:
  1. **Current Culture** - survey to representative sample of company population
     - Question: "¿Qué se espera hoy que hagas para ser parte de?"
  2. **Desired Culture** - same survey but to executive team (C-Level, 7-15 people)
     - Question: "¿Qué se debería esperar en el futuro que las personas hagan para ser parte de?"
- The gap between these photos drives all subsequent conversations
- Qualitative interviews supplement the OCI data

### The Core Message to Leaders
> "La cultura se construye a partir de mensajes que todos los miembros de la organización reciben de distintas fuentes, pero principalmente del comportamiento que observan en sus líderes."

Translation: Culture is built from messages everyone receives, primarily from observing leader behavior.

### Sequential Cascading Through Leadership Levels

1. **C-Level starts first** - they initiate transformation
2. **N-2 (Gerentes) follows with 1-2 month delay**
3. **N-3 and beyond** - continues cascading

**Why the intentional delay?**
> "Si tú eres un C-Level y alguien que te reporta a ti lo empezara exactamente al mismo tiempo... se pierde la persona que te reporta a ti la oportunidad de volver a ti cuando él hace su experiencia de aprendizaje encontrándote a ti ya con un poco de camino recorrido."

The subordinate needs to find their leader with some path already walked - someone who can "walk the talk" and support their difficulties.

## 3. Three Intervention Types in Culture Projects

### Type 1: Workshops (Talleres)
- Transfer of knowledge, exercises, practices
- Training modality
- Participants think about challenging situations to apply learnings
- **Limitation:** "El taller por sí mismo nunca es suficiente, es una instancia que ayuda pero no alcanza"

### Type 2: Coaching
- Anchors workshop learning to "la realidad más concreta y específica de cada participante"
- Without coaching, learning "se evapora en 48 horas" (evaporates in 48 hours)
- Always starts with individual diagnostic (LSI or Civitool)

### Type 3: Facilitaciones (Two Sub-types)

**Type A: In-Situ Business Meeting Facilitation**
- Facilitator observes real business meetings
- Has permission to intervene and provide feedback
- Points out: listening issues, interruptions, presenting opinions as facts, unchecked inferences, victim language
- Example intervention: "Ruy, te escuché decir que tú no estabas pudiendo llegar a tus objetivos a causa de que Dolo no te manda tal cosa. ¿Cómo podrías decir eso mismo de un modo más protagónico?"

**Type B: High Performing Teams (HPT)**
- Structured team development sessions
- Pre-designed agenda agreed with team leader
- Session 1: Clarify objectives, connect team goals to individual goals, establish purpose
- Session 2: Define processes - who makes decisions about what, empowerment clarity, decision mechanisms
- Can be a product in itself or part of culture projects

## 4. Leadership Development Programs

When not framed as culture transformation but as leadership development:

- Coaching included as "anchor" for workshop learning
- Timing critical: coaching sessions should occur close in time to workshops
- May include team composite LSI (aggregate of individual styles)
- Can address inter-team conflicts (e.g., commercial vs. systems teams)

## 5. Individual Coaching Process Components

### Pre-Coaching Setup

**Reunion Cero (Meeting Zero)**
- Meeting between coach, coachee, and coachee's boss
- Sets objectives together
- Boss states expectations: "Espero que mejores tu capacidad de escucha" or "que mejores tu gestión del tiempo"
- These become reference points throughout the process
- Coach helps clarify and document these objectives

**Diagnostic Instruments**
- **LSI (Life Styles Inventory)** - preferred for culture projects because it speaks same language as OCI
- **CB (Conscious Business) Assessment Tool** - increasingly used
- Horacio's strong opinion: "En cualquier lugar que tú encajes el coaching, sea como proceso autónomo o como parte de otra cosa, si le falta el diagnóstico individual, te estás perdiendo una enorme oportunidad de tocar la fibra de la conciencia de la persona"

### The Individual-to-Culture Connection

When doing LSI feedback in culture context:
> "Ahora vamos a sacar una foto que habla el mismo idioma de la foto que tuviste de la cultura de la compañía, pero que ahora nos va a estar mostrando cómo tú estás contribuyendo con tu comportamiento individual a esa foto de la compañía."

The coachee sees: How am I contributing to current culture? What must I change to contribute to desired culture?

### Mid-Process Touch-Base
- Some processes include sponsor check-ins
- Helps ensure alignment with organizational objectives

### Final Tripartite Meeting
- Coach, coachee, and boss review progress
- Validates development against initial objectives

## 6. Horacio's Suggestion for AI

### Qualitative Data Capture via Interviews

Horacio suggests exploring AI-enabled qualitative diagnostic:

> "Yo no dejaría de explorar cómo tecnológicamente se puede hacer un relevamiento cualitativo... es lo más poderoso que yo he visto para que la persona transite el coaching con conciencia y compromiso."

**Options discussed:**
1. Audio interviews with AI asking questions → transcription → AI processes for insights
2. Text questionnaire (though Horacio notes this "debilita las chances de que te den respuestas buenas")

**Why it matters:**
- Quantitative instruments (LSI, CB Assessment) lack "matices y textura y de ejemplos específicos"
- Interviews with referents (people proposed by the coachee) provide rich context
- Confidentiality maintained about who said what
- Testimonials and quotes in reports create powerful awareness moments:

> "Entonces ahí sí, la persona quizás cae en la cuenta del impacto que están provocando su comportamiento sobre los demás."

---

## Key Implications for Stoic AI Product

1. **Diagnostic before coaching** - Don't skip the awareness-building phase
2. **Context matters** - Understanding where coaching fits (standalone vs. program) affects approach
3. **Connect individual to system** - Help users see how their behavior contributes to larger patterns
4. **Consider qualitative input** - AI-conducted interviews could be more powerful than questionnaires alone
5. **Memory across sessions** - Coaching is "una larga conversación repartida en seis momentos" not isolated events
